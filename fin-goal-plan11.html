      Frequently Asked Questions

      • What if I don't know all my financial goals right now?
        That's perfectly normal. Goal planning is an iterative process. We start with the goals you're certain about (like retirement, children's education) and build a framework that can accommodate new goals as they emerge. Your plan is living document that evolves with your life circumstances.
      • How often should I review my financial goals?
        We recommend annual reviews to track progress and make necessary adjustments. However, major life events (job change, marriage, birth of child, inheritance, health issues) should trigger immediate reviews to ensure your plan remains aligned with your new reality.
      • What if I can't afford the required monthly investments right now?
        Goal planning helps you understand the gap between your current capacity and requirements. We then work on strategies like prioritizing critical goals first, planning for income growth, optimizing expenses, or adjusting timelines where possible. The plan shows you what's needed‚Äîimplementation can be phased based on your circumstances.
      • How accurate are inflation-adjusted projections?
        While we can't predict exact inflation rates, we use historical data and multiple scenarios (low, moderate, high inflation) to create a realistic range. This approach ensures you're prepared for various economic conditions rather than relying on a single assumption that could prove inaccurate.
      • Can I adjust my goals later if priorities change?
        Absolutely. Financial goal planning is flexible by design. Life circumstances change‚Äîcareer shifts, health situations, family dynamics‚Äîand your financial plan should adapt accordingly. We help you reassess priorities and reallocate resources as needed while keeping you on track for your most important objectives.
